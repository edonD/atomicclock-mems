Energy Levels & Hyperfine Structure

The 6,834,682,610.904 Hz gap between F=2 and F=1 is the clock transition. Two laser sidebands straddle this gap to create Coherent Population Trapping (CPT).

Why one valence electron?

The inner 36 electrons fill shells 1–4 completely (the krypton core). The 37th electron must go to n=5, subshell 5s. With only one outer electron, Rb behaves like a simple two-level-like quantum system — ideal for laser manipulation.

Nuclear spin I = 3/2

The Rb-87 nucleus has spin I = 3/2. Combined with the 5s electron spin J = 1/2, the total angular momentum F = I±J = 2 or 1. These two hyperfine ground states are the clock levels, separated by 6.835 GHz.

Pauli Exclusion

No two electrons share the same four quantum numbers (n, l, ml, ms). Each orbital holds at most 2 electrons with opposite spins (↑↓). The 5s¹ electron is unpaired — that lone spin is what we exploit.

Valence electron 5s¹

n=5, l=0, ml=0, ms=+½. The 5→5 electric-dipole transition is forbidden (Δl=0). Transitions use D1 (794.7 nm) and D2 (780.2 nm) lines: 5S → 5P via photon absorption.

Quantum Number Quick Reference

n — Principal quantum number

Determines shell (energy level). n = 1,2,3,4,5 for Rb-87. Shell letters: K,L,M,N,O. Energy ∝ −1/n².

l — Azimuthal quantum number

Determines subshell shape. l = 0(s), 1(p), 2(d), 3(f). l ranges 0 to n−1. s=sphere, p=dumbbell, d=clover.

ml — Magnetic quantum number

Orientation of orbital in space. ml = −l to +l. In a magnetic field each ml has a slightly different energy (Zeeman effect).

ms — Spin quantum number

Electron intrinsic spin: +½ (up ↑) or −½ (down ↓). The 5s¹ valence electron is unpaired (only one spin present).

F — Total hyperfine angular momentum

F = J + I where J = electron total angular momentum, I = nuclear spin = 3/2. Ground state: F=1 (lower) and F=2 (upper).

mF — Hyperfine magnetic sublevel

mF = −F to +F. The clock transition uses |F=1, mF=0⟩ ↔ |F=2, mF=0⟩, which is first-order insensitive to magnetic fields.
